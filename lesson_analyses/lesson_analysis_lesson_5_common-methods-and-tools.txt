### Analysis of Student Messages from the "Common Methods and Tools" Lesson

#### 1. Concepts or Topics Students are **Struggling** to Understand:
- **Understanding the Return Value of Methods**: Students are confused about methods like `pop()` that both remove an element from an array and return that element. #### 4. Summary of Main Areas of Struggle and Understanding:
- **Struggles**:
  - **Understanding Return Values**: Students struggle with methods that not only modify data (e.g., by removing or adding elements) but also return values, which complicates their understanding of the flow of data.
  - **Distinguishing Between Similar Methods**: There is confusion among students regarding the differences in functionality between similar methods (e.g., `push()` vs. `pop()`).

- **Understanding**:
  - **Basic Functionality**: Students show a good grasp of how to use several built-in methods in JavaScript, especially with practical examples.
  - **Resource Utilization**: Students are aware of how to leverage online documentation and resources for further learning, indicating a proactive approach to their coding education.

### Conclusion
Overall, while students are grasping the basic concepts and functionalities of JavaScript methods, they face challenges in understanding the intricacies of method return values and distinguishing between similar methods. Providing more examples and analogies to clarify these aspects could enhance their learning experience.
